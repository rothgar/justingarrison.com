## Description

The "starter cluster" has evolved from the simple days of kube-up to powering the largest platforms in the world. Kubernetes is the application interface developers expect, but it doesn't fit everywhere. Justin has spent years trying to meet that challenge. He's built some of the smallest and largest clusters in the world using old, new, and custom-build hardware. He'll share lessons learned from putting Kubernetes into places it wasn't originally designed to run—and how those experiences are shaping a simpler path forward with Talos Linux.
